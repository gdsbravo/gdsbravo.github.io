Ceiling Price is the max limit set by the government on the price of a medicine.

Problem - Imagine you have diabetes and you need Insulin to avoid any serious health problems. The reasonable cost of Insulin should be Rs 1,000 per month. But companies that make insulin take advantage of patients like you and increase its price to Rs 10,000 per month.

Solution -  The government intervenes by setting a ceiling price of Rs 1500 per month. This means that no company can increase the price of insulin above Rs 1500. This protects the customers from exploitation.
